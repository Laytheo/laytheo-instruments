## Description

Economics investigates how individuals, firms, governments, and societies allocate scarce resources to meet competing ends. It is conventionally divided into microeconomics (the behavior of individual agents and markets) and macroeconomics (aggregate phenomena such as national output, inflation, and unemployment). Its major schools include classical, neoclassical, Keynesian, Austrian, and monetarist traditions. The discipline employs mathematical modeling, statistical analysis, and historical and institutional study, and continually engages with questions raised by ethics, political philosophy, and political science.
