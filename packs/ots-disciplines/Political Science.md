## Description

Political Science investigates the structures, processes, and behavior of political life: governments, elections, public policy, international relations, and political institutions. Its major subfields include comparative politics, international relations, political theory, public administration, and public policy. Distinct from political philosophy (which is normatively oriented and continuous with philosophy), political science as a modern discipline emphasizes empirical methods, though political theory within it preserves continuity with the philosophical tradition.
