## Description

Pneumatology examines the identity of the Holy Spirit as the third person of the Trinity and the Spirit's work in creation, revelation, the life of Christ, the Church, and the sanctification of believers. The classical doctrine of the Spirit's full divinity was articulated against the Pneumatomachi in the fourth century and definitively affirmed at the Council of Constantinople (381). Pneumatology has received renewed systematic attention in the twentieth and twenty-first centuries through Pentecostal-charismatic movements and ecumenical dialogue.
