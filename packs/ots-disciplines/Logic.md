## Description

Logic studies the forms of correct reasoning: which arguments preserve truth from premises to conclusion, which inferences are valid, and what makes them so. Its history runs from Aristotle's syllogistic and the Stoic propositional logic, through medieval logical refinements, to the development of modern symbolic logic by Frege, Russell, and others, and on to twentieth-century work in modal logic, intuitionism, and the logical foundations of mathematics. Logic serves as a tool used across philosophy, mathematics, computer science, and any discipline concerned with rigorous argument.
