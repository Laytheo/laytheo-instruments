## Description

Historical Theology examines how Christian doctrine has been articulated, contested, and refined from the apostolic age to the present. It treats theological development as an object of study, attending to the contexts, controversies, and figures that shaped doctrinal formulation. Distinct from Church History (which focuses on the institutional and social history of the Church), Historical Theology focuses on the history of theological ideas themselves.
