## Description

Sociology investigates the patterns, structures, and processes of social life: how individuals are shaped by groups and institutions, how societies hold together and change, and how power, inequality, and meaning are produced and distributed. Founded as a distinct discipline in the nineteenth century by Comte, Marx, Durkheim, and Weber, it employs both quantitative and qualitative methods and includes major subfields such as the sociology of religion, economic sociology, political sociology, and cultural sociology.
