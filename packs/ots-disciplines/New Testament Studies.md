## Description

New Testament Studies investigates the twenty-seven books of the New Testament using historical, literary, philological, and theological methods. It attends to the historical Jesus, the formation of the Gospels, the Pauline corpus, the development of early Christianity, and the relationship of the New Testament to Second Temple Judaism and the Greco-Roman world. The field has been shaped by form criticism, redaction criticism, the various quests for the historical Jesus, and the New Perspective on Paul.
