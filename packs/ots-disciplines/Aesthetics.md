## Description

Aesthetics treats the nature of beauty, the sublime, taste, and aesthetic judgment, as well as the philosophy of art (its definition, interpretation, and value). It addresses questions such as whether beauty is objective or subjective, what distinguishes art from non-art, and how aesthetic experience relates to moral and intellectual experience. Theological aesthetics (notably von Balthasar's) extends the discipline to the consideration of divine beauty and revelation as a primary mode of God's self-disclosure.
