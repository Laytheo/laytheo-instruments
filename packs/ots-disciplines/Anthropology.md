## Description

Anthropology investigates human beings holistically across time and place. As constituted especially in the American tradition, it comprises four fields: cultural anthropology (the study of contemporary human cultures), archaeology (past cultures through material remains), linguistic anthropology (language as a cultural phenomenon), and biological or physical anthropology (human evolution and variation). Its central methods include ethnography and participant observation, and its core concerns include kinship, ritual, religion, exchange, and the meaning of cultural difference. Distinct from theological anthropology, which treats humanity in light of revelation.
