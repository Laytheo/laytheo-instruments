## Description

Psychology investigates mental processes and behavior in individuals and groups, drawing on experimental, clinical, observational, and increasingly neuroscientific methods. Its major subfields include cognitive psychology, developmental psychology, social psychology, clinical psychology, and personality psychology. The discipline emerged from philosophy in the late nineteenth century with figures like Wundt and James, was reshaped by the psychoanalytic tradition, the behaviorist revolution, and the cognitive turn, and continues to develop in dialogue with neuroscience and the philosophy of mind.
