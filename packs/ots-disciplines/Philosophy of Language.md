## Description

Philosophy of Language treats how words and sentences come to mean what they do, how reference is fixed, how speakers communicate, and how language relates to thought and reality. Central topics include the theory of meaning (sense and reference, truth-conditional semantics), reference and naming (descriptivism, direct reference, rigid designation), speech acts, pragmatics, and the relation of language to mind and world. The discipline became central to twentieth-century analytic philosophy, which often took linguistic analysis to be philosophy's primary method.
