## Description

Literary Criticism encompasses the interpretation and evaluation of literary works, attending to their form, meaning, historical context, and aesthetic and moral significance. The tradition runs from Aristotle's Poetics through medieval and Renaissance commentary, into the diverse modern schools: formalism, New Criticism, structuralism, deconstruction, reader-response, historicism, and various politically inflected approaches. As an academic discipline it overlaps significantly with literary theory (its more conceptual mode) and with hermeneutics, philology, and the history of ideas.
