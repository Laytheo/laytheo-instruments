## Description

Liturgical Theology proceeds from the conviction that the Church's worship is itself theological, encoding and transmitting doctrine through prayer, sacrament, and ritual action. The discipline often invokes the principle lex orandi, lex credendi (the law of prayer is the law of belief) to argue that liturgy is not merely the expression of doctrine arrived at independently but a primary locus of theological knowledge. It draws on patristics, sacramental theology, and historical liturgy.
