## Description

Philosophy of Religion brings the methods of philosophy to bear on questions raised by religious belief: the existence and nature of God, the problem of evil, the relation of faith and reason, the epistemology of religious experience, the meaningfulness of religious language, and the relation of science and religion. It is distinguished from theology by its working from premises available to natural reason rather than from revelation, though the boundary between the two is porous. Its modern revival, particularly in analytic philosophy, has produced significant work in natural theology and theistic metaphysics.
