## Description

Archaeology investigates past human societies through the systematic recovery, recording, and interpretation of their material remains: settlements, artifacts, ecofacts, inscriptions, and human remains. It encompasses prehistoric and historic archaeology, with major specializations including classical archaeology, biblical archaeology, ancient Near Eastern archaeology, and processual and post-processual approaches in method and theory. The discipline supplies essential evidence for periods and questions where textual sources are sparse or absent and continually reshapes the reconstruction of ancient and historical worlds.
