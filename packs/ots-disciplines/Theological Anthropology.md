## Description

Theological Anthropology examines what it means to be human in light of divine revelation: the imago Dei, the constitution of body and soul, the entrance and effects of sin, the human capacity for grace, and the eschatological destiny of persons. It engages with and is distinct from philosophical anthropology and the human sciences, proceeding from theological commitments while remaining attentive to what other disciplines disclose about human nature.
