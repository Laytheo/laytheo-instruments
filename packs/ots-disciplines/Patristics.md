## Description

Patristics studies the Christian writers of roughly the first eight centuries, from the Apostolic Fathers through John of Damascus in the East and through Bede or Isidore in the West. It encompasses textual editing, translation, theological exposition, and historical contextualization of these figures and their works. The discipline is foundational for Historical Theology and is increasingly drawn upon by systematic and biblical theologians as a resource for retrieval.
