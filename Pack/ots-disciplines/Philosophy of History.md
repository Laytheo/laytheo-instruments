## Description

Philosophy of History encompasses two related inquiries: the analytic or critical philosophy of history (concerned with the methods, objectivity, and epistemology of historical knowledge) and the speculative philosophy of history (concerned with whether history as a whole has a direction, pattern, or meaning). The latter has produced major proposals from Augustine's City of God through Hegel's account of Spirit, Marx's historical materialism, and various theological and post-theological readings of history. The discipline overlaps significantly with theology, particularly eschatology and the doctrine of providence.
