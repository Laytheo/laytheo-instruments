## Description

Philology is the historical and comparative study of texts, encompassing close attention to language (vocabulary, grammar, etymology), textual transmission, literary form, and cultural context. It is the older umbrella discipline from which modern linguistics, textual criticism, and much of literary studies emerged in the nineteenth century. The classical philological tradition (Greek and Latin) remains foundational for biblical studies, patristics, and medieval studies, where the careful reading of difficult texts in their original languages is indispensable.
