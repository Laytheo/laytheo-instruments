## Description

Ecclesiology examines the Church as the people of God, the body of Christ, and the temple of the Holy Spirit. It treats questions of the Church's marks (one, holy, catholic, apostolic), its visibility and invisibility, its relation to Israel, its sacramental life, its ministerial offices, its authority, and its mission in the world. Ecclesiology is a primary site of confessional difference between Catholic, Orthodox, and Protestant traditions and a central concern of modern ecumenical theology.
