## Description

Linguistics investigates the structure, function, history, and acquisition of human language. Its core subfields include phonetics and phonology (sounds), morphology (word structure), syntax (sentence structure), semantics (meaning), and pragmatics (use). Applied subfields include historical linguistics, sociolinguistics, psycholinguistics, and computational linguistics. The discipline took its modern form with Saussure's distinction between langue and parole and was further transformed by Chomsky's generative grammar in the mid-twentieth century.
