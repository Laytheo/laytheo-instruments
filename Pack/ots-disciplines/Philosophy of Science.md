## Description

Philosophy of Science examines the nature of scientific knowledge, the structure of scientific theories, the criteria for theory choice, the demarcation between science and non-science, and the metaphysical commitments implicit in scientific practice. Major topics include induction and confirmation, falsification, paradigms and scientific revolutions, scientific realism versus anti-realism, explanation, laws of nature, and the role of values in science. The discipline includes general philosophy of science and philosophies of particular sciences (physics, biology, mathematics, social science).
