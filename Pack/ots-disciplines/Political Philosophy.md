## Description

Political Philosophy examines the foundational questions of political life: the nature and legitimacy of authority, the basis and limits of government, the relation of individual and community, justice, rights, liberty, and equality. It treats both the descriptive question of what political life is and the normative question of how it ought to be ordered. The tradition runs from Plato's Republic and Aristotle's Politics through medieval reflection on political authority, the social contract tradition (Hobbes, Locke, Rousseau), to contemporary debates over liberalism, communitarianism, and political theology.
