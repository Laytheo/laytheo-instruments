## Description

Ancient Near Eastern Studies investigates the civilizations of Mesopotamia, Egypt, Anatolia, the Levant, and Persia from roughly the fourth millennium BC through the Persian period. The field encompasses Assyriology (Akkadian, Sumerian), Egyptology, Hittitology, Northwest Semitic studies (Ugaritic, Aramaic, Hebrew), and the archaeology and history of these regions. It is closely allied with Old Testament Studies, providing the linguistic, literary, and cultural context within which the Hebrew Bible took shape.
