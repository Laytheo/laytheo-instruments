## Description

Christology addresses the identity of Jesus Christ as fully God and fully man, the relation of his two natures in one person, and the meaning of his life, death, resurrection, and ascension. The classical formulations were forged in the Christological controversies of the fourth through seventh centuries, culminating in the definitions of Nicaea, Ephesus, Chalcedon, and Constantinople III. Modern Christology continues to engage these classical formulations alongside questions raised by historical-critical biblical scholarship and contemporary philosophy.
