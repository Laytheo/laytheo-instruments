## Description

Textual Criticism examines the manuscripts and other witnesses to a text in order to evaluate variant readings and reconstruct the most likely original wording. Applied to the Bible, it works with thousands of Hebrew, Greek, Latin, Syriac, and other manuscripts, employing both internal criteria (which reading best explains the others) and external criteria (the age, geographic distribution, and quality of witnesses). The discipline is foundational for biblical translation and exegesis and has its own history of method, schools, and major critical editions.
