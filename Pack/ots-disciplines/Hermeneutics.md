## Description

Hermeneutics treats the principles and conditions of interpretation: how meaning is conveyed by texts, how readers understand or fail to understand, and how historical and cultural distance is bridged. It originated as a discipline of biblical and legal interpretation but was developed in the modern period (Schleiermacher, Dilthey) into a general theory of understanding, and in the twentieth century (Heidegger, Gadamer, Ricoeur) into a philosophical account of human meaning-making as such. Theological hermeneutics applies these concerns specifically to Scripture.
