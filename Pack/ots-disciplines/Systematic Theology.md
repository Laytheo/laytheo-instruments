## Description

Systematic Theology arranges the content of Christian belief into ordered loci (such as the doctrine of God, of Christ, of salvation, of the Church) and seeks the internal coherence of the whole. It draws on biblical exegesis, historical theology, and philosophical reasoning to articulate doctrine in a manner faithful to Scripture and responsive to its own time. Major works in the genre include Aquinas's Summa Theologiae, Calvin's Institutes, and Barth's Church Dogmatics.
