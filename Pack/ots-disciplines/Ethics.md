## Description

Ethics treats the questions of right action, the good life, virtue, duty, and value. It is traditionally divided into metaethics (the nature and grounding of moral claims), normative ethics (which actions are right and why), and applied ethics (specific moral questions in medicine, business, politics, and elsewhere). The major normative traditions include virtue ethics (descended from Aristotle), deontology (associated with Kant), and consequentialism (especially utilitarian forms). Christian ethics engages these traditions from within a theological frame.
