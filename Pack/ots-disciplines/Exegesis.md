## Description

Exegesis is the practice of drawing out the meaning of a text through careful attention to its language, grammar, historical context, literary form, and place within a larger corpus. In biblical exegesis this includes work in the original languages (Hebrew, Aramaic, Greek), engagement with text-critical questions, and attention to the rhetorical and theological aims of the human and divine author. Exegesis is distinguished from eisegesis (reading meaning into a text) and is the indispensable ground on which biblical and systematic theology build.
