## Description

Classical History investigates the civilizations of ancient Greece and Rome from approximately the eighth century BC through late antiquity, drawing on literary sources, inscriptions, archaeology, numismatics, and papyrology. It encompasses the political, military, social, intellectual, and religious history of the Greek city-states, the Hellenistic kingdoms, the Roman Republic and Empire, and the transition to late antiquity and the early medieval world. The field is closely connected to classical philology, archaeology, and patristics, particularly in its treatment of late antiquity.
