## Description

Metaphysics treats the most basic questions about what exists and what it is to exist: substance and accident, matter and form, essence and existence, universals and particulars, causation, time, modality, and the nature of God. The term derives from the arrangement of Aristotle's works (the books that came after the Physics) but came to designate the inquiry into being as such (ontology) and the first principles of things. Metaphysics has been variously embraced, transformed, and rejected across the history of philosophy, from medieval scholasticism through Kant's critique to twentieth-century revivals in analytic philosophy.
