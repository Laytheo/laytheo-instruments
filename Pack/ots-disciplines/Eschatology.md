## Description

Eschatology treats the final destiny of creation, humanity, and the Church: the return of Christ, resurrection, judgment, the new heavens and new earth, and the intermediate state. It encompasses both individual eschatology (death, judgment, heaven, hell) and cosmic eschatology (the renewal of all things). Twentieth-century theology saw a major recovery of eschatology as central rather than peripheral to Christian doctrine, reframing it as the horizon within which all other doctrines must be understood.
