## Description

Church History investigates the institutional, social, intellectual, and spiritual life of the Christian Church from the apostolic age to the present. It treats the spread of Christianity, the development of ecclesiastical structures, the great councils and controversies, monasticism, the medieval Church, the Reformation, missionary expansion, and the modern Church in its many forms. Distinct from Historical Theology (which focuses on the history of doctrine), Church History attends to the broader life of the Christian community in its historical contexts.
