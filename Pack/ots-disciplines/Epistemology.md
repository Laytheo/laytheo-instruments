## Description

Epistemology examines what knowledge is, how it is acquired, what justifies belief, and what the limits of human understanding are. Its central questions include the definition of knowledge (the classical analysis as justified true belief and the Gettier challenges to it), the sources of knowledge (perception, reason, testimony, memory), the structure of justification (foundationalism, coherentism), and the response to skepticism. The discipline shapes and is shaped by work in metaphysics, philosophy of mind, and philosophy of science.
