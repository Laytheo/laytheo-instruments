## Description

Philosophy of Mind treats the nature of mental states, the relation of mind to body, the character of consciousness, the structure of intentionality, and the place of the mental in the natural world. Major positions include substance dualism (Descartes), various forms of physicalism (identity theory, functionalism, eliminativism), and emergentism. The discipline engages closely with cognitive science, neuroscience, and artificial intelligence, while retaining distinctly philosophical questions about subjective experience, mental causation, and personal identity.
